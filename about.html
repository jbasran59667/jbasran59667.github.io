My projects begin as a self confessed study in hindsight juxtaposing culture and society in the present to the past, exploring how we got here and asking how things might be different. I tend to give this method flight by scrutinising my own surroundings and my own hybridity of culture.

Born and raised in multi-cultural London I further my design process by taking inspiration from the complexities and quirks of modern British society. By including the dichotomy of cultures engrained here, my work always divulges a sense of criticism of an English pastime of looking through rose tinted glass using the irony of famous British satire.

Growing up in rainy grey weather has imparted on me a love of outerwear. Creating coats and jackets as key pieces and using technical and traditional coating fabrics in unassuming garments gives my work a utilitarian aesthetic focusing on silhouettes, structure and an injection of colour into the monotone on London life.
